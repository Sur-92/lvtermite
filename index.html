What's Actually Pressuring Homes Here Right Now

Skip the scare stories. Here's the honest, local picture for the Lebanon Valley this year — and what it means for your home.

A dry year drives termites to your foundation

The eastern subterranean termite is the one that costs real money here — and drought doesn't slow it down, it concentrates it. Colonies chase dependable moisture: downspouts, hose bibs, crawlspaces, and watered beds against the house. This is the summer to walk your foundation lines and fix grading and drainage.

It's the quiet pests, not the headlines

The spotted lanternfly everyone panicked about is down more than 90% across our region. The steady threat underneath is the unglamorous one: subterranean termites and carpenter ants working through older local housing stock — usually in damp, poorly-drained wood.

An elevated mosquito & West Nile summer

Dry summers favor the house mosquito that carries West Nile, and vector counts are already up locally. Empty anything holding standing water weekly — clogged gutters, buckets, plant saucers, tarps, and forgotten containers around the yard.

Ticks & Lyme stay a year-round risk

Pennsylvania leads the country for Lyme disease, and the blacklegged (deer) tick is in every county. If your property backs to woods or a field edge, keep the lawn trimmed, clear leaf litter, and hold a mulch or gravel buffer between the tree line and where kids and pets play.

The thread tying it together: where water is scarce this year, the pests that need it relocate to the man-made moisture around your buildings — exactly where you don't want them. That makes summer 2026 a smart time for a foundation-to-eaves once-over. We'll take a look — free. Local conditions per Penn State Extension and PA DEP / county mosquito & tick programs.
